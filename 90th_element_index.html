Movement One
The Dream
1954 – 1974
I
Geneva, August 1954
August 8 – 20, 1954
At the world's first atomic energy conference, Homi Bhabha does the arithmetic on India's uranium reserves and draws three boxes connected by arrows in a hotel notebook.
II
Trombay, 1955–1960
1955 – 1960
A young engineer named Arjun Nair arrives at Bhabha's new laboratory on a hill above Bombay harbour, and India's first reactor goes critical on a Monday morning.
III
Oak Ridge, 1965–1969
June 1965 – November 1969
In Tennessee, Alvin Weinberg's molten salt reactor works perfectly — and is shut down anyway, because the question was never whether it worked.
IV
Mont Blanc, January 1966
January 24, 1966
Bhabha boards a flight to Geneva with a briefcase full of calculations he expects to discuss next week; Arjun inherits the unfinished folder left on his desk.
Movement Two
The Long Silence
1974 – 2011
V
Pokhran, May 1974
May 18, 1974
India's nuclear test closes the international doors that the three-stage programme needed open; Arjun watches from Trombay as his son Vikram begins looking toward Canada.
VI
The Invisible Decades
1974 – 2000
Three snapshots across twenty-six years of work that history does not record — the calculations, the decisions, the patience that accumulates a programme one Tuesday at a time.
VII
Washington and Vienna, 1995–2005
1995 – 2005
Three women in three cities — a diplomat in Delhi, a physicist in Vienna, an official in Washington — each holding a piece of the same picture, in rooms not yet built for the conversation that would assemble it.
VIII
Shanghai and Wuwei, 2011
March – December 2011
A young Chinese physicist named Mei Lin downloads the fifty-year-old Oak Ridge results in forty-one seconds, wrestles with the question of inheritance, and boards a train to the Gobi Desert.
Movement Three
The Awakening
2011 – 2026
IX
Kalpakkam, 2014–2022
2014 – 2022
Arjun comes out of retirement to solve a weld problem nobody else can find, holds a thorium rod in his gloved hands in a clean room, and takes a video call from his son in Vancouver during a pandemic.
X
Wuwei, October 2023
October 11, 2023
Mei Lin's reactor achieves criticality — the first molten salt reactor anywhere in the world since Oak Ridge in 1965; in Albuquerque, the last man who was there in 1965 reads the news over his second cup of coffee.
XI
Wuwei and Kalpakkam, 2025–2026
November 2025 – March 2026
China confirms the first thorium-to-uranium conversion; Loeb writes to Mei Lin; Arjun waits in Chennai for Rajan's call; the fuel loading at Kalpakkam reaches completion.
XII
Kalpakkam, April 6, 2026
April 6, 2026
The Prototype Fast Breeder Reactor achieves first criticality; seventy-two years close in a single morning; a child on the Kerala beach holds dark sand in her hands and watches it fall.
